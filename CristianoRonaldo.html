Cristano Ronaldo

Childhood

Cristiano Ronaldo grew up in extreme poverty on the island of Madeira, living in a cramped home where he shared a single room with all three of his siblings. He used football as an escape from his difficult circumstances, often playing in the streets with makeshift balls until he joined his first local club at age seven. Despite facing a heart condition and the pain of leaving his family at just twelve years old to pursue his dreams in Lisbon, his relentless work ethic eventually transformed him into a global superstar.

Career

Cristiano Ronaldo’s professional career began at Sporting CP before he skyrocketed to global fame during his first stint at Manchester United, where he won his first Ballon d'Or and Champions League title. He then spent nine record-breaking seasons at Real Madrid, becoming the club's all-time leading scorer and securing four more European crowns, followed by successful spells at Juventus and a return to Old Trafford. Now playing for Al Nassr in Saudi Arabia, he remains the highest-scoring male international player in history and is widely regarded as one of the greatest athletes to ever play the game.

Achievements

Cristiano Ronaldo has amassed a record-breaking collection of both team and individual honours, headlined by five Ballon d'Or awards and five UEFA Champions League titles won across his time in Manchester and Madrid. On the international stage, he famously led Portugal to their first major trophies at Euro 2016 and the UEFA Nations League in 2019 and 2025. As of late April 2026, he stands as the all-time leading goalscorer in men's football history with 969 senior career goals, and he is currently chasing the unprecedented milestone of 1,000 professional goals.
